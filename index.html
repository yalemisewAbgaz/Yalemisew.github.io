About Me

Welcome to the website of Dr. Yalemisew Abgaz!

Yalemisew is an assistant profssor in the School of Computing, Dublin City University, under the DCU Futures program for data literacy and analytics.

  • Yalemisew's primary research interest includes the application of The Semantic Web technologies (Ontologies and Knowledge Graphs) in Software Engineering, Natural Language Processing, Digital Humanities, Artificial Intelligence and other related areas.

  • Yalemisew is also associated with the Future Software Systems Architecture (FSSA) Project under Lero . His primary interest covers the investigation of the migration of monolithic applications to microservices architecture.

  • Yalemisew has been a principal investigator affiliated with ChIA Project in ADAPT Centre funded by the Austrian Academy of Sciences. Yalemisew has also worked as an associate lecturer in the School of Computing at National College of Ireland (NCI). He delivered undergraduate and postgraduate modules and supervised six MSc students.
  • Yalemisew reveived his PhD in Computer Science, from Dublin City University. His Phd research topic is “Analysing the impacts of ontology changes in content-based systems” under the supervision of Dr Claus Pahl. Since 2014, Yalemisew has involved in research projects funded by FP7 (Maynooth University), SFI (DCU), EI (DCU), Go!digital (DCU) and other projects.

Introduction to Database Systems - CA218

School of Computing, Dublin City University, Ireland

2021/22 Semester 1.

The aim of this module is to provide students with an introduction to and an overview of database systems including database design, entity Relationship data modelling, the relational model of data and SQL, as well as an overview of some database products.

Introduction to Programming - CA146/CA297

School of Computing, Dublin City University, Ireland

2020/21 Semester 2.

This module introduces students the basics of programming including variables, control flow (branching, repetition), functions, and files. Students learn to write their own programs using Python by following lectures and several lab tasks demonstrating each topic.

Algorithms and Advanced Programming

School of Computing, NCI, Ireland

2020/21 Semester 2 and semester 3

Algorithms and advanced programming covers advanced concepts including algorithm analysis, multi-threading, exception handling, data sanitisation and advanced file manipulation. This module is delivered using Java.
